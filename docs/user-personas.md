# User Personas — AbhiIterates.OS

Five primary user archetypes derived from target audience research.
Every product decision must trace back to at least one of these personas.

---

## Persona 1 — Arjun Mehta | Engineering Student

**Age:** 20 | **Year:** 3rd Year B.Tech CSE | **College:** Tier-2 Engineering College, Pune
**Device:** Android phone (primary), laptop (study sessions) | **Internet:** College WiFi + 4G

### Background
Arjun is a computer science student preparing for placements while keeping up with his semester coursework. He is moderately technical — he uses GitHub, knows some Python, and has used ChatGPT. He juggles 6 subjects, competitive programming practice, and project work simultaneously. His study material comes from professors (PDFs shared on WhatsApp), Telegram channels, and paid courses.

### Goals
- Pass all 6 subjects with a decent CGPA
- Crack a product or SDE interview by final year
- Build a project that looks good on his resume
- Avoid repeating the chaos of last semester (lost notes, missed deadlines)

### Pain Points
- Notes are in 4 places: WhatsApp downloads, Google Drive, email attachments, and handwritten notebooks
- PDFs shared by professors have no bookmarks or annotations — he has to start from scratch every time
- ChatGPT doesn't know his syllabus — he has to paste text into it manually
- Paid notes he buys get leaked in his friend group within hours
- He has to switch between 6-8 apps in a single study session

### Motivations
- Placement anxiety drives urgency — wants to study smart, not just hard
- Peer comparison — sees batch mates getting organized, feels the pressure
- Wants to demonstrate discipline and consistency to future employers

### Technical Knowledge
Medium. Comfortable with web apps, file uploads, cloud storage concepts. Will not read documentation but expects UI to be self-explanatory.

### Daily Workflow
```
7:00 AM  — Checks WhatsApp for professor PDF shares
8:00 AM  — Opens PDF in phone gallery viewer (no annotations)
10:00 AM — College classes
2:00 PM  — Study session: opens 3 PDFs, a YouTube video, and ChatGPT simultaneously
6:00 PM  — Competitive programming
10:00 PM — Pastes chapter into ChatGPT, asks for summary
11:30 PM — Scrambles to find last week's notes before sleeping
```

### Frustrations
- "I spend more time finding my notes than reading them."
- "My AI doesn't know my textbook."
- "I bought notes for ₹200 and my friend got them for free in 10 minutes."

### Opportunities for AbhiIterates.OS
- Central library eliminates search frustration
- AI chat trained on his uploaded PDFs solves the context problem
- Signed URLs + DRM watermarking protects seller, builds trust in marketplace
- Highlights and bookmarks persist — no re-reading from scratch

## Persona 2 — Sneha Iyer | Medical Student

**Age:** 22 | **Year:** 3rd Year MBBS | **College:** Government Medical College, Chennai
**Device:** iPad (primary study device), iPhone | **Internet:** Hostel WiFi

### Background
Sneha is an MBBS student with one of the most text-heavy syllabi in any Indian degree. She studies from 400-page anatomy atlases, Harrison's Internal Medicine, and faculty-prepared PDFs. She has developed her own annotation system in GoodNotes but struggles to use AI tools because her study material is highly specialized — generic ChatGPT gives wrong information for medical topics.

### Goals
- Ace her university exams and NEET PG preparation
- Build a personal, annotated reference library for clinical years
- Find reliable, subject-specific notes without paying for garbage

### Pain Points
- Medical PDFs are enormous (50–400 pages) — phone apps crash or load slowly
- GoodNotes doesn't have AI — she has to manually summarize chapters
- Medical knowledge is high-stakes — she cannot trust AI that hallucinates
- Finding good notes by subject and topic is nearly impossible on generic platforms
- Collaborative studying with batch mates requires sharing entire files, not sections

### Motivations
- Medical study is long — any efficiency gain compounds over 5.5 years
- Accuracy is non-negotiable — the AI must cite page and source
- Collaborative revision sessions are how her peer group studies for exams

### Technical Knowledge
Low-medium. Expert Apple ecosystem user. Comfortable with GoodNotes, Anki. Uncomfortable with complex settings or developer tools.

### Daily Workflow
```
6:00 AM  — Morning revision with GoodNotes
9:00 AM  — Hospital clinical rounds
2:00 PM  — Theory classes
5:00 PM  — Self-study: annotates PDFs, highlights key points
9:00 PM  — Revision with batch mates via WhatsApp voice notes
11:00 PM — Creates handwritten flashcards from her annotations
```

### Frustrations
- "My GoodNotes annotations don't help me when I need to quickly find something."
- "AI gives me wrong drug doses — I can't trust it."
- "I've re-read the same chapter four times because I can't find my highlights from last month."

### Opportunities for AbhiIterates.OS
- AI answers grounded in the specific PDF she is reading (RAG) — solves hallucination
- Persistent highlights with search enables finding any annotation instantly
- AI-generated MCQs from her own study material for revision
- Collaboration on shared PDF sessions with live annotations

## Persona 3 — Rohan Shah | Commerce Student

**Age:** 19 | **Year:** 2nd Year B.Com | **College:** SRCC, Delhi
**Device:** Windows laptop | **Internet:** Home broadband

### Background
Rohan is a B.Com student who also preparing for CA Foundation. He deals with dense financial accounting, economics, and business law content. He buys notes from seniors every semester — often overpriced and poorly organized. He is the type of student who will pay for quality if it saves him time.

### Goals
- Clear CA Foundation in the first attempt
- Organize notes by subject, chapter, and topic
- Find authentic, quality study material without wasting money on bad notes

### Pain Points
- Notes quality varies wildly — no way to verify before buying
- Study material is scattered across CA Institute website, Telegram, and printed notes
- Business law has frequent amendments — notes go stale quickly
- No search inside PDFs he has bought

### Motivations
- Cost savings are important — will invest in tools that demonstrably save time or money
- Trusts peer reviews and ratings heavily before buying anything

### Technical Knowledge
Low. Uses basic Windows apps, MS Office. Not comfortable with complex tools. Expects everything to work like Google Docs or Notion.

### Daily Workflow
```
9:00 AM  — Revision from printed notes
11:00 AM — CA coaching classes
3:00 PM  — Self-study with PDF textbooks
7:00 PM  — YouTube lectures
10:00 PM — Short notes preparation before sleep
```

### Frustrations
- "I bought notes for ₹500 and they were copy-pasted from the textbook."
- "There's no way to know if notes are good until I've already paid."
- "I can't search inside any of my PDFs — I have to scroll through everything."

### Opportunities for AbhiIterates.OS
- Ratings, reviews, and preview before purchase builds trust
- Full-text PDF search eliminates manual scrolling
- Subject-organized library with chapter tagging for CA structure

## Persona 4 — Priya Nair | Content Creator (Notes Seller)

**Age:** 24 | **Post-graduate** | **Background:** Topped her batch, now preparing for UPSC
**Side income:** Sells handwritten notes on Instagram DMs and Telegram

### Background
Priya creates genuinely high-quality, handwritten notes and study guides. She sells them informally via Instagram posts and Telegram groups for ₹100–500 per set. She struggles with payments (UPI only, manual tracking), her notes get leaked constantly, and she has no analytics to understand what is selling. She wants to go professional but does not know how.

### Goals
- Generate ₹20,000–50,000/month from notes sales
- Stop notes leaking after every sale
- Understand which topics are most in demand
- Build a brand and recurring customer base

### Pain Points
- Managing payments, delivery, and customers manually is a part-time job
- Files leak within hours of every sale — zero protection
- No analytics: doesn't know which notes sell, who buys, what subjects are trending
- Instagram and Telegram are not designed for this use case
- Tax and financial tracking is completely manual

### Motivations
- Financial independence — notes selling is her primary income while preparing for UPSC
- Recognition — wants to build a brand identity as "the best UPSC notes creator"
- Scalability — wants a system that sells without her active involvement

### Technical Knowledge
Medium. Comfortable with Instagram, Canva, Telegram bots, and UPI. Not a developer. Expects a dashboard that does not require technical knowledge.

### Daily Workflow
```
6:00 AM  — UPSC study
10:00 AM — Creates note content (handwritten or typed)
2:00 PM  — Photographs/scans notes, uploads to Telegram
3:00 PM  — Responds to payment DMs, shares links manually
5:00 PM  — Customer support: sends missing pages, handles complaints
8:00 PM  — UPSC study
```

### Frustrations
- "I worked 10 hours on a note set and someone shared it in a free group the same evening."
- "I don't know if 50 or 5000 people have my notes at this point."
- "I made ₹3000 this month but I have no idea which notes brought it in."

### Opportunities for AbhiIterates.OS
- Marketplace with signed URL delivery eliminates manual distribution
- Earnings dashboard with per-resource analytics
- DRM watermarking deters casual sharing (not foolproof, but a strong deterrent)
- Creator profile page builds brand identity

## Persona 5 — Prof. Rajesh Verma | Teacher / Professor

**Age:** 48 | **Role:** Assistant Professor, Mechanical Engineering | **College:** NIT Jaipur

### Background
Prof. Verma has been teaching for 18 years. He shares study material with students via email attachments and a WhatsApp group. He has been asked by the department to "go digital" but the college's LMS (Moodle) is unusable and he does not have time to learn a new tool. He wants something that is simple enough to use in 10 minutes.

### Goals
- Share course PDFs with students without managing email attachments
- Students should be able to highlight and annotate without printing
- Optionally monetize his supplementary lecture notes

### Pain Points
- WhatsApp groups get chaotic — resource finding is impossible
- Email attachment limits prevent sharing large PDFs
- No way to track if students are actually reading the material
- The college LMS is outdated and poorly maintained

### Motivations
- Student success is genuine motivation — wants better engagement with material
- Low learning curve required — will abandon tool if onboarding takes more than 15 minutes

### Technical Knowledge
Low. Email, WhatsApp, basic Google Drive. Will not read documentation. Needs extremely simple UX with no jargon.

### Daily Workflow
```
9:00 AM  — Lectures
1:00 PM  — Research / paper writing
4:00 PM  — Responds to student emails
6:00 PM  — Prepares next day's material
```

### Opportunities for AbhiIterates.OS
- Course creation with bulk PDF upload
- Share via link — students do not need to create accounts to preview
- Optional monetization for supplementary content
- Simple analytics: who opened what, average read time
